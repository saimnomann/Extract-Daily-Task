# Study Schedule Planner

## Instructions

When creating a study schedule, follow this systematic approach to create an effective and balanced plan.

### 1. Gather Information
- Collect all subjects/topics to study
- Note available study time per day/week
- Identify any deadlines or exam dates
- Determine difficulty level of each subject
- Consider any preferred study times or constraints

### 2. Assess Priority and Difficulty
- High Priority: Subjects with upcoming exams or critical importance
- Medium Priority: Regular subjects with consistent importance
- Low Priority: Subjects that are less urgent or easier
- Assign difficulty levels (Easy, Medium, Hard) for each subject

### 3. Calculate Time Distribution
- Total available study time รท Number of subjects = Base time per subject
- Adjust based on priority:
  - High Priority: 1.5x base time
  - Medium Priority: 1x base time
  - Low Priority: 0.75x base time
- Consider difficulty - add 25% more time for difficult subjects

### 4. Create Balanced Schedule
- Distribute study sessions throughout the week
- Avoid back-to-back sessions of the same subject
- Include short breaks between sessions (5-10 minutes)
- Schedule difficult subjects during peak focus times
- Mix subjects with different difficulty levels

### 5. Present in Clear Format
- Create a visual schedule (table or calendar format)
- Include session duration and subject
- Add notes on prioritization strategy
- Provide tips for effective studying
- Suggest review sessions and practice time

### 6. Add Flexibility and Review
- Include catch-up time for missed sessions
- Recommend weekly review sessions
- Suggest adjustment strategies if falling behind
- Provide tips for maintaining the schedule

## Time Distribution Rules

### Priority Multipliers
- **High Priority**: 1.5x base time
- **Medium Priority**: 1x base time
- **Low Priority**: 0.75x base time

### Difficulty Adjustments
- **Easy**: No adjustment (100%)
- **Medium**: +25% time
- **Hard**: +50% time

### Session Length Guidelines
- **Concentration Sessions**: 45-90 minutes
- **Breaks**: 5-10 minutes between sessions
- **Long Breaks**: 30+ minutes after 2-3 sessions
- **Maximum Daily**: 6-8 hours total study time

## Study Tips and Best Practices

### Effective Study Techniques
1. **Pomodoro Technique**: 25 minutes study, 5 minutes break
2. **Spaced Repetition**: Review material at increasing intervals
3. **Active Recall**: Test yourself instead of just re-reading
4. **Interleaving**: Mix different subjects/topics in one session
5. **Concept Mapping**: Visual connections between ideas

### Schedule Maintenance
1. **Weekly Review**: Adjust schedule based on progress
2. **Flexibility**: Allow 1-2 catch-up sessions per week
3. **Consistency**: Study at the same times each day
4. **Balance**: Include rest and recreational activities
5. **Tracking**: Monitor actual vs planned study time

### Subject-Specific Strategies
- **Mathematics**: Daily practice, focus on problem-solving
- **Science**: Concept understanding before memorization
- **Languages**: Daily exposure, practice speaking/writing
- **Programming**: Hands-on coding, build projects
- **Theory Subjects**: Active reading, note-taking, discussion
