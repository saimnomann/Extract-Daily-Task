# Professional Email Writer

Instructions for writing professional emails that maintain a polite and clear tone while being ready to send.

## Instructions

When writing a professional email, follow these steps:

### 1. Gather Essential Information
Before writing, collect:
- **Recipient's name and title** (if known)
- **Purpose of the email** (main topic)
- **Key points to include** (3-5 maximum)
- **Desired action/outcome** (if any)
- **Tone** (formal, semi-formal, casual professional)

### 2. Email Structure Template
```
Subject: [Clear and concise subject line]

Dear [Recipient Name],

[Opening line - state purpose immediately]

[Body paragraph 1 - context and main point]

[Body paragraph 2 - supporting details or action items]

[Body paragraph 3 - conclusion or next steps]

[Closing and signature]
```

### 3. Subject Line Guidelines
- Keep under 60 characters
- Be specific and informative
- Include relevant keywords
- Avoid all caps or excessive punctuation

### 4. Opening Lines
For different purposes:
- **Follow-up**: "Following up on our conversation about..."
- **Introduction**: "I hope this email finds you well. I'm writing to..."
- **Request**: "I would like to request information about..."
- **Update**: "This email is to provide an update on..."
- **Thank you**: "Thank you for your time and consideration regarding..."

### 5. Body Content Rules
- **One idea per paragraph**
- **Use bullet points** for lists (3+ items)
- **Keep paragraphs short** (3-4 lines)
- **Use clear, simple language**
- **Avoid jargon** unless recipient understands it
- **Be concise but complete**

### 6. Closing Guidelines
For different tones:
- **Formal**: "Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to your response."
- **Semi-formal**: "Please let me know if you have any questions. I appreciate your help."
- **Action-oriented**: "I would appreciate it if you could [specific action] by [date]."

## Examples

### Example 1: Meeting Request
```
Subject: Meeting Request: Project Alpha Discussion

Dear John,

I hope this email finds you well. I would like to schedule a meeting to discuss the upcoming Project Alpha launch.

The purpose of our meeting would be to:
- Review the current project timeline
- Discuss resource allocation
- Address any potential challenges

Would you be available for a 30-minute call next Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon? Please let me know what time works best for you.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to connecting.

Best regards,
Jane Smith
Project Manager
```

### Example 2: Follow-up Email
```
Subject: Following Up: Budget Approval Request

Dear Sarah,

Following up on my email from last week regarding the Q3 budget request.

I wanted to check if you've had a chance to review the proposal. If you need any additional information or clarification, please don't hesitate to let me know.

The key points from the proposal include:
- Marketing campaign budget: $50,000
- Timeline: September-December 2024
- Expected ROI: 150%

Could you please provide an update on the approval process by end of week?

Thank you for your assistance.

Sincerely,
Michael Chen
Finance Department
```

### Example 3: Thank You Note
```
Subject: Thank You for Your Presentation

Dear Dr. Williams,

Thank you for your insightful presentation on artificial intelligence trends at yesterday's conference.

Your discussion on machine learning applications in healthcare was particularly valuable. The examples you shared about predictive analytics in patient care provided new perspectives that I'm excited to explore further.

I would appreciate the opportunity to connect again and discuss potential collaboration opportunities. Please feel free to reach out at your convenience.

Thank you again for sharing your expertise.

Best regards,
Alex Rodriguez
Technology Innovations Lab
```

## Best Practices

### Do's
- ✅ Use the recipient's name
- ✅ Keep emails concise (under 200 words)
- ✅ Proofread for typos and grammar
- ✅ Include clear call-to-action
- ✅ Use professional email signature

### Don'ts
- ❌ Use excessive exclamation points
- � Write overly long paragraphs
- ❌ Use informal language (unless appropriate)
- ❌ Send without proofreading
- ❌ Forget to include contact information

## Common Email Scenarios

### 1. Requesting Information
- Be specific about what you need
- Explain why you need it
- Set reasonable deadline
- Offer to provide additional context

### 2. Apologizing
- Acknowledge the issue immediately
- Take responsibility
- Explain what happened (briefly)
- Offer solution or next steps

### 3. Declining a Request
- Thank the person for considering you
- Give clear reason (briefly)
- Offer alternative if possible
- Maintain positive relationship

### 4. Providing Feedback
- Start with positive observation
- Be specific and constructive
- Focus on behavior, not person
- Offer suggestions for improvement
